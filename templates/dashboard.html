Understanding Our Coaching Approaches

CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)

CBT focuses on identifying and changing unhelpful thought patterns and behaviors around food. This evidence-based approach helps you understand the connection between your thoughts, feelings, and eating habits.

DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy)

DBT combines mindfulness with emotional regulation techniques. It helps you develop skills for mindful eating, managing food-related stress, and building a balanced approach to nutrition.

ACT (Acceptance & Commitment Therapy)

ACT helps you develop psychological flexibility by accepting your thoughts and feelings while committing to behavior change aligned with your values. It focuses on mindfulness, acceptance, and value-driven actions.

IPT (Interpersonal Therapy)

IPT focuses on how relationships and communication patterns influence eating behaviors. This approach addresses social eating, emotional support, and resolving interpersonal issues that may trigger emotional eating.
